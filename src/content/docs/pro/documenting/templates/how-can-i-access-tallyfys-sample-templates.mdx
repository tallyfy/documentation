## How to access and use sample templates

Tallyfy offers ready-made [template](/products/pro/documenting/templates/) examples in our libraries. These can help you quickly set up common [processes](/products/pro/tracking-and-tasks/processes/) based on standard ways of working. You can then change them to fit your exact needs.

### Benefits of using sample Templates
- **Get started fast**: Set up processes quickly without starting from zero.
- **Use good examples**: Learn from well-designed workflow examples.
- **Be consistent**: Helps keep your processes similar across your company.
- **Learn how templates work**: See real examples to understand how to build your own.
- **Save time**: Set up common [processes](/products/pro/tracking-and-tasks/processes/) faster.

### Finding sample Templates

You can find Tallyfy's sample templates in two main places:

1. **Tallyfy's Public Template Library**
   - Go to [Tallyfy's Public Library](https://go.tallyfy.com/public/library).
   - Look through templates organized by category (like HR, Marketing, etc.).
   - Preview any template before you decide to use it.
   - Click to import a template directly into your Tallyfy account.

2. **Inside Your Tallyfy Account**
   - Go to the **Templates** section in Tallyfy.
   - Click **Import** or **New Template**.
   - Choose the **Sample Templates** option.
   - Browse the available categories and choose a template.

### Changing a sample Template to fit your needs

Once you import a sample template into your account:

<Steps>
1. Look through the steps and overall flow.
2. Change step names and descriptions to use your company's wording.
3. Update who is assigned to tasks to match your team.
4. Add or remove steps if needed.
5. Change the [form fields](/products/pro/tracking-and-tasks/tasks/what-are-form-fields-in-tallyfy/) to collect the information you need.
6. Adjust deadlines based on how long things should take for you.
7. Test the template by [launching](/products/pro/launching/) a test [process](/products/pro/tracking-and-tasks/processes/).
</Steps>

### Tips for organizing your templates

- Put templates into folders based on department or type of process.
- Use clear and consistent names for your templates.
- Add tags to make templates easier to find and filter.
- Write down any important changes you made to sample templates so others understand.
