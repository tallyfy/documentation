## How to clear the Safari browser cache

Clearing your Safari cache can fix loading issues, display problems, or outdated content in Tallyfy. The steps are different for Mac and iOS devices.

### Mac computers

<Steps>
1. Open Safari on your Mac.
2. Click **Safari** in the menu bar (top left of the screen).
3. Choose **Settings...** (or **Preferences...** in older macOS versions) from the dropdown menu.
4. Click the **Advanced** tab.
5. Check the box for **Show Develop menu in menu bar** (or **Show features for web developers**) at the bottom.
6. Close the Settings/Preferences window.
7. Click **Develop** in the menu bar.
8. Select **Empty Caches** from the dropdown menu.
</Steps>

For a more complete clearing (removes cookies and other data too):

<Steps>
1. Click **Safari** in the menu bar.
2. Choose **Settings...** (or **Preferences...**).
3. Click the **Privacy** tab.
4. Click **Manage Website Data...**.
5. Click **Remove All**.
6. Confirm by clicking **Remove Now** in the pop-up.
</Steps>

### iOS devices (iPhone/iPad)

<Steps>
1. Open the **Settings** app on your iPhone or iPad.
2. Scroll down and tap **Safari**.
3. Scroll down again and tap **Clear History and Website Data**.
4. Tap **Clear History and Data** to confirm.
</Steps>

After clearing the cache, close and reopen Safari before trying Tallyfy again.

:::note[Safari on Mac: keyboard shortcut]
You can quickly clear Safari's cache on Mac using the keyboard shortcut `Command+Option+E` after enabling the Develop menu (step 5 in the first Mac section).
:::

If clearing your cache doesn't fix your Tallyfy issue, please [contact Tallyfy support](https://tallyfy.com/contact-us/) for more help.
