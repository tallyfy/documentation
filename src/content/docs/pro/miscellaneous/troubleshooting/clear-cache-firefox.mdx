## How can I clear Firefox's browser cache to resolve Tallyfy issues?

Clearing Firefox's cache eliminates performance problems, loading errors, and outdated content issues in Tallyfy by removing temporary files that may be corrupted or outdated. This process ensures Firefox downloads fresh content from Tallyfy's servers.

<Steps>
1. Launch Firefox on your computer.
2. Click the menu button (â˜°) located in the top right corner.
3. Select **Settings** from the dropdown menu options.
4. Navigate to the **Privacy & Security** panel in the left sidebar.
5. Scroll down to locate the **Cookies and Site Data** section.
6. Click the **Clear Data...** button to open clearing options.
7. Enable the **Cookies and Site Data** checkbox.
8. Enable the **Cached Web Content** checkbox.
9. Click the **Clear** button to initiate the clearing process.
10. In the confirmation dialog, click **Clear Now** to complete the action.
</Steps>

### What should I do after clearing Firefox's cache?

After successfully clearing the cache, completely close Firefox and reopen it before accessing Tallyfy again. This ensures all cached data is properly removed and Firefox starts with a clean slate.

:::note[Alternative method: keyboard shortcut]
You can also open the 'Clear Recent History' dialog using `Ctrl+Shift+Delete` (Windows/Linux) or `Command+Shift+Delete` (Mac). Ensure 'Cache' is checked before clearing.
:::

### What if clearing cache doesn't resolve my Tallyfy issue?

If clearing Firefox's cache doesn't resolve your Tallyfy problems, please [contact Tallyfy support](https://tallyfy.com/contact-us/) for additional troubleshooting assistance and specialized help.
